## Clinical Philosophy
- **Avoids diagnostics** — prefers general labels like "back pain" over pathoanatomical diagnosis
- Integration of rehab and training is the core angle
- Evidence-based but practical — research must translate to actionable content

## Anatomy Focus
- **Back:** Both lumbar AND thoracic spine
- **Hips:** Hip flexor/psoas, glutes, adductors, deep rotators
- Whole body but these are the pillars

## Research Interests
### Primary
- Systematic reviews (priority)
- EMG research — exercise ranking AND myth-busting (e.g., psoas isn't the villain)
- Pain science, tissue healing timelines, how they intersect with strength programming
- Load progression, training modifications during rehab
- Myth-busting / contrarian findings

### Myth Topics That Fire Her Up
- "You need a strong core to protect your back"
- "Stretching prevents injury"
- "Posture causes pain"
- "You shouldn't deadlift with a disc bulge"
- "Glute med weakness causes knee valgus"
- (and similar traditional rehab dogma)

### Adjacent Topics — Include
- Pain neuroscience / central sensitization
- Sleep, stress, psychosocial factors in recovery
- Nutrition for tissue healing / performance
- Biomechanics / movement variability
- **Broaden over time** — as app learns more about her, expand into new relevant topics

### What Makes a Paper "Scroll-Stopping"
- Large sample challenging a common protocol
- New EMG data on understudied exercises
- Systematic review that reverses established findings
- Direct clinical applicability that can be demoed in a reel
- **Ideal:** A paper with a scroll-stopping finding convertible into an engaging 20-second reel

## Content Format Sweet Spot
- 20-second reels with a single punchy finding
- Research → reel pipeline is the goal
